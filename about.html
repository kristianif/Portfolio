Background

Built things before I could name what I was doing.

I'm a mechanical engineering student at the University of Idaho going into my senior year. My education has given me the vocabulary for what I've always done naturally: understand systems, diagnose problems, and build solutions that last.

My work spans vehicle restoration, precision engine rebuilding, and hands-on fabrication. I bring the same methodical thinking to a frame-off Jeep restoration that I bring to engineering coursework — thorough, detail-oriented, and not done until it's right.

Engineering school didn't teach me to work with my hands. It taught me to understand why what I was already doing worked — and how to do it better.
