Project Overview

The 1973 Jeep CJ5 is a full frame-off restoration — meaning every component has been removed, inspected, repaired or replaced, and reassembled from the ground up. The goal isn't just to make it run; it's to build something that will outlast any modern vehicle off the lot.

This project demands working knowledge of virtually every automotive system at once: structural frame integrity, suspension geometry, drivetrain tolerances, brake hydraulics, wiring, and body work. There's no single discipline — it's all of them, simultaneously.

Phase 01

Frame Inspection & Repair

The foundation of any frame-off build. The CJ5 frame was stripped bare, inspected for cracks, rust, and deformation, then repaired and treated. Getting this right means everything built on top of it is solid.

Phase 02

Suspension Rebuild

Full suspension rebuild — new leaf springs, shocks, and hardware throughout. Alignment geometry checked against factory spec. The goal: a suspension that handles off-road loads without compromise.

Phase 03

Drivetrain Overhaul

Transfer case, axles, differentials, and driveshafts — each inspected, measured, and rebuilt to spec. The CJ5's drivetrain was designed to be simple and repairable. The goal is to prove that.

Phase 04

Electrical Systems

50-year-old wiring doesn't get a pass. Full electrical re-wire: new harness, corrected grounding, modern fuse block, and clean routing. Done right so it doesn't become a fire hazard.
